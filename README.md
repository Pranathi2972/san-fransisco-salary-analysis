# San Francisco Employee Salary Analysis

## Overview
This project explores the salary data of San Francisco city employees to understand how compensation varies across different job roles and years. By analyzing base pay, overtime, and benefits, the project aims to identify patterns in public sector salaries and highlight the factors that influence total employee earnings.

---

## Objective
The main goals of this project are:
- To study how salaries are distributed among San Francisco employees  
- To identify job roles with higher compensation  
- To analyze the effect of overtime and benefits on total pay  
- To observe salary trends over multiple years  

---

## Dataset
- **Source:** San Francisco Public Employee Salary Dataset  
- **Size:** Over 300,000 employee records  
- **Key Columns:**
  - Employee Name  
  - Job Title  
  - Base Pay  
  - Overtime Pay  
  - Other Pay  
  - Benefits  
  - Total Pay  
  - Total Pay with Benefits  
  - Year  

The dataset contains real-world public salary data and required cleaning due to mixed data types.

---

## Approach
The project was completed using the following steps:
1. Loaded and inspected the dataset  
2. Converted salary-related columns to numeric format  
3. Handled missing and invalid values  
4. Performed exploratory data analysis using visualizations  
5. Interpreted results to draw meaningful insights  

---

## Analysis & Insights
- Most employee salaries fall within a limited range, with a small number of employees earning significantly higher amounts  
- Certain job roles consistently receive higher compensation  
- Overtime pay plays a major role in increasing total earnings  
- Overall employee compensation shows an upward trend across years
